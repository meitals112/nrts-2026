Artist Statement

Self-regulation is intimate: you feel something, attempt to understand it, and that understanding shapes how you act. NRTS proposes that this process is unnecessary.

NRTS — Neutral Response Training System is a speculative interactive installation staged as a clinical training service. Participants enter a fake clinic, sign a consent form, undergo biometric calibration, and are exposed to an uncomfortable conflict scenario. The system measures facial expression, vocal pitch, response timing, valence, arousal, and dominance. It does not ask what the participant feels. It trains what the participant produces.

The installation examines an assumption embedded in affective computing and behavioral management systems: that interaction can be regulated without engaging the interior at all. The question is not “can emotion be suppressed?” but “does emotion need to exist?”

In this future, workplaces schedule neutral-response training annually — a compliance requirement, processed like any other. At home, conflict is not resolved; it is outperformed. The household functions as an economic unit. No one is asked to stop feeling. They are trained to produce correct responses regardless of what they feel. The interior becomes operationally irrelevant. Feeling is not forbidden. It is simply costly.

Participants receive a score. They are certified, or they are not.

Project Description

In this future, most labor has been replaced by automated systems. The Department of Labour, responding to structural unemployment, commissioned research to identify what human workers could offer that automated systems could not — and what factors predicted human retention in hybrid workplaces. The findings were narrow. Emotional variability predicted conflict, absenteeism, and workflow disruption. Emotional stability predicted retention. The Department formalized the outcome: to remain certifiably employable, a human worker must demonstrate behavioral stability within measurable parameters. NRTS issues that certification.

The logic extends beyond the workplace. Emotional conflict — whether in a performance review or a domestic dispute — produces quantifiable downstream costs. Divorce generates legal proceedings, housing instability, and welfare dependency. Parental emotional dysregulation generates unnecessary conflict with children and predicts psychiatric intervention. The government’s interest in stable families is fiscal, not moral. The family is infrastructure. Infrastructure requires maintenance.

NRTS — Neutral Response Training System is a speculative interactive installation staged as a fake clinical training service, operating as an accredited provider under the National Behavioral Standards Authority. Participants arrive at a simulated clinic, sign a consent form, and undergo biometric calibration. The system exposes them to an uncomfortable conflict scenario and instructs them to practice a prescribed neutral response. Facial expression, vocal pitch, response timing, valence, arousal, and dominance are measured continuously. A coherence/neutrality score is calculated. A certification label is printed. The system does not ask what the participant feels. It measures what the participant produces.

The neuroscience is instructive. Automatic emotional responses — the amygdala firing within 20 milliseconds of a stimulus — cannot be trained away. What NRTS targets is the subsequent step: the prefrontal activation that converts that response into a conscious feeling, analyzes its meaning, and attempts regulation. That processing is expensive. It consumes attention, creates delay, and occupies capacity that could be allocated elsewhere. The system does not fight the interior. It routes around it. The amygdala fires. The signal is registered. Nothing is done with it.

In a world where everyone produces calibrated responses, you can no longer tell the difference between someone who is fine and someone performing fine. Eventually neither can they. The distinction between feeling and output collapses — not because feeling disappears, but because it stops mattering to the interaction. What you actually feel becomes private data with no interface. The texture of this future is not hostile. It is frictionlessly empty.

Technical Information

NRTS runs on a dedicated workstation with webcam and microphone input. Facial landmark tracking and emotion recognition are implemented using MediaPipe and a pre-trained affect classification model. Voice analysis captures pitch, flow, and stability in real time via Web Audio API. VAD-inspired metrics — valence, arousal, dominance — are computed from combined biometric inputs and aggregated into a coherence/neutrality score. A Brother QL-700 label printer outputs printed certification upon session completion. The installation also includes a custom-authored introductory film, physical consent form station, and institutional signage. All components operate as a unified bureaucratic system.

Meital Geva is a graduate artist and researcher working at the intersection of speculative design, institutional critique, and interactive systems. Her practice examines how technological infrastructures extend bureaucratic logic into affective and bodily experience — particularly in workplace, domestic, and compliance contexts. Her work takes the form of fake institutions, scored interactions, and systems that measure what bodies produce rather than what people feel. She is currently completing graduate studies at Bezalel Academy of Arts and Design. NRTS — Neutral Response Training System is her most recent installation.

“Things are simpler when you do not feel.”
